Hello! I'm a 3rd year PhD student in EECS at MIT, co-advised by David Sontag in MIT CSAIL and Gaddy Getz at the Broad Institute.

Before starting my PhD, I went to Stern College of Yeshiva University for undergrad, where I majored in Physics and minored in Computer Science (and got an Associates Degree in Judaic studies!) and then studied Biomedical Engineering for my MS at Columbia Engineering. I also worked on the Molecular Profiling team at Regeneron Pharmaceuticals for 2 years, which sparked my fascination with algorithms for genomic data.

Research

I am excited about our ability to use machine learning to better understand health and disease, especially in ways that can be impactful for patients. I focus on developing machine learning algorithms that can utilize clinical and genomic data for this purpose, with a particular focus on genomics and cancer.
